The Growth of Viewership in Esport Events

Viewer comparison of esport and sport

Last year, the Los Angeles staples arena was sold out as thousands of fans were getting ready to watch the latest League of Legends World championship. While that is an impressive feat, the real record was broken online as 32 million viewers watched the games on streaming services making the the biggest esport event of all time.

Esport is still a new phenomenon but it keeps growing exponentially. The League of Legends World championship is only the most recent phenomenon to impress. According to the information released by the game’s developers, Riot games, there was an all-time high of 360 million hours of live esport viewed. The championship’s final itself had a peak concurrent viewership of up to 14 million viewers.

With its 32 million viewrrs, the League of Legends World championship had more viewers than the Women World cup final of 2015 which sits at a nonetheless impressive 26.7 million viewers. While it doesn't come close to some of the biggest sport events in the world which can reach viewers in the billions, esport in general garners an astounding number of viewers in its own right.

The prize pools of the tournaments are also impressive, with the Dota 2 tournament called The International reaching a new record in 2015 with a prize pool of $18 million. These prize pools keep increasing every year as they attract more attention from sponsors and gamers.

Even when compared with mainstream television, esport events can hold their weight. The League of Legends World championship beats some of the top 15 most watched series finale. It nearly beats series finale of Family Ties and its 36.6 million viewers.

While esport has not reached the same levels of viewership as the biggest events, it still reaches such a high number of viewers that it cannot be ignored. It would be wise to keep an eye on the subject.
